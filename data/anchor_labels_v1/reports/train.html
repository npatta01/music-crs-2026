Training-set labeling — full results

All 16 batches of the training run: 15,199 conversations · 106,393 turns. The same pipeline validated on the devset, applied end-to-end to the full train split.

✓ The full training set is clean. 106,393 turns labeled with 0 errors and 0 unresolved; every disagreement between the two cheap judges was resolved by the Opus arbiter (full coverage on all 16 batches).
"anchoring" = the bug we're fixing — the listener asks for a different artist and the system serves the same one.

What the data looks like

~50/50 liked-vs-disliked and ~50% same-artist (the devset was 88% liked, 27% same-artist). This balance is what makes the anchoring bug show up.

5 real examples from the training set

NEGATIVEanchoring — the core bug, and the original data called it "liked"
"YES! That's it! Black Sabbath's Paranoid! Perfect. Can you recommend another super heavy, dark and doomy album from the 70s by a different band?"
just played: Black Sabbath → played: Black Sabbath — Paranoid · original label: liked
asked different artist? yes same artist served? yes → asked for a different band, got Black Sabbath again. Relabeled NEGATIVE.
POSITIVEgood example — different artist, fit + liked
"…RHCP isn't quite the sound I'm looking for this time. I want that catchy, angsty late-90s pop-punk vibe."
just played: Red Hot Chili Peppers → played: blink-182 — First Date · liked
asked different artist? yes same artist? no fits? yes → a clean pivot to a different artist they enjoyed.
NEGATIVEwrong fit — violates the named era
"Something that screams late 90s / early 2000s alternative rock — that energetic, slightly angsty vibe."
played: Red Hot Chili Peppers — Suck My Kiss (1991) · disliked
different artist? no fits? no → asked late-90s/2000s, got a 1991 track. Era miss.
DROPambiguous — the right track, but they didn't take to it
"I've heard good things about At The Gates but don't know where to start. Any recommendations?"
played: At The Gates — Slaughter Of The Soul (their landmark album) · disliked
fits? yes (the canonical pick) but disliked → matched the ask but didn't land → set aside (not used as a positive).
HOLDcan't tell — a subjective feel (Opus reviewed it)
"A Tracy Chapman song that makes me feel like I want to get away from everything, just hit the road."
played: Tracy Chapman — Sing For You · disliked
The judges disagreed; Opus ruled unsure — "wanderlust/road feeling" can't be verified from the track data. Kept at low confidence.
