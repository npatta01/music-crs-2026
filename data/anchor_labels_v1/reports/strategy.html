Using the anchoring labels — the strategy

Turning the POSITIVE / NEGATIVE / DROP / HOLD labels into retriever + reranker training, and the one catch that decides how you use them.

⚠ The catch

On the devset official scoring, the ground-truth track for a turn is the track the system played — the exact track our labels grade. So our labels call that GT “bad” on 58% of train turns (anchoring or wrong-fit). Demoting it = fighting devset nDCG.
Scope: this is the devset script. The blind A/B sets that decide the leaderboard are not this script (extra metrics kept server-side) and are likely quality-weighted — they may favor the fix. Don’t over-fit to devset nDCG.

Where the value lands in the score

Composite = 0.50·nDCG@20 + 0.10·diversity + 0.10·lexical + 0.30·judge

nDCG 0.50 — in tension ⚠ judge 0.30 — helps ✓ div 0.10 ✓ lex 0.10

The anchoring fix fights the 0.50 nDCG term on the devset (it demotes the played GT), but helps the 0.40 judge+diversity that rewards respecting the “different artist” ask. Put the labels where they help, keep them out of where they hurt.

The one rule: rank it down, don’t ban it

✗ pool removal (ban)

Treat “bad” as “never retrieve this track.” The track is deleted from results.

User: “…another doomy 70s album by a different band.” → ban Black Sabbath.
But the devset GT is Black Sabbath → you deleted the answer → nDCG = 0.
✓ ranking hard-negative

Treat “bad” as a training example: rank other artists above it. The track stays retrievable.

Pull “different band” tracks to the top; Black Sabbath still sits at, say, rank 8 → slate respects the ask and the GT stays in the 20.

What I’m suggesting — the plan

①
Keep the SUBMITTED reranker label-pure. It’s trained on label = (track == played GT). Feeding anchor-NEGATIVE grades in would invert the metric’s own GT. Don’t. protects devset
②
Use the labels in the PRODUCT retriever — “add, don’t subtract.” Pull different-artist tracks into the top-1000 pool alongside the anchored GT; anchoring tracks as moderate hard-negatives. Recall- and diversity-positive, metric-neutral. devset-safe
③
Spend the labels on the JUDGE + diversity axes (response generation + slate). That’s ~40% of the composite, rewards respecting the “different artist” ask, and is the axis the blind eval most likely weights. helps blind too
④
Measure anchoring on a held-out anchoring-rich slice, not dev nDCG. Anchoring is only 5% of the devset — the fix is invisible (or looks negative) on dev nDCG by construction.
